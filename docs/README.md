# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to OctoAcme's project management documentation hub. This folder explains how our teams deliver work from idea to impact using a consistent lifecycle: **Initiation → Planning → Execution → Release → Retrospective**. Use this README as your starting point, then open each guide for the detailed checklists, templates, and operating practices.

OctoAcme uses a structured delivery model to keep projects aligned and incremental. During **Initiation**, teams validate the business problem, define success metrics, and align stakeholders. In **Planning**, teams prioritize backlog items, define acceptance criteria and dependencies, and set milestones and release expectations. In **Execution**, teams deliver in small, testable increments while tracking progress, risks, and blockers. **Release** formalizes production readiness through deployment checklists, smoke tests, and stakeholder communication, and **Retrospective** turns outcomes and incidents into actionable improvements.

Delivery is shared across three core personas with clear ownership boundaries. **Project Managers** coordinate timelines, risks, dependencies, and cross-team communication. **Product Managers** define outcomes, prioritize roadmap and backlog work, and track measurable business impact. **Developers** implement and review solutions, maintain quality, and collaborate on estimation and technical decisions. These roles work as a system so customer value, technical quality, and operational visibility stay connected.

Communication and quality are built into the weekly rhythm. Teams use regular standups and delivery syncs to surface blockers early, plus recurring stakeholder updates for transparency and decision-making. Risk management is maintained through a living risk register and escalation paths from team triage to sponsor-level intervention when needed. Quality assurance combines unit, integration, and smoke testing with CI checks and security scanning, while retrospectives ensure teams continuously improve how they plan and deliver.
